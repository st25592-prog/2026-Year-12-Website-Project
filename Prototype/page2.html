Origins of Rugby League

Rugby league was founded in 1895 in Huddersfield, England, after a major split from rugby union. The disagreement began because many northern English clubs believed players should receive compensation for time taken off work due to injuries or travel for matches. Rugby union officials strongly opposed paying players, insisting that the sport remain strictly amateur. As a result, twenty-two clubs broke away and formed the Northern Rugby Football Union, which later became known as rugby league.

Over time, the new code of rugby began to develop its own identity. Rules were gradually changed to make the game faster and more entertaining. The number of players was reduced, and the play-the-ball system was introduced.

Growth of the Game

After its formation in England, rugby league quickly expanded beyond its original northern communities. In the early 1900s, the sport spread to Australia and New Zealand, where it gained strong support.

Australia became one of the strongest rugby league nations, with club competitions forming in major cities. Television broadcasts later played a huge role in increasing the sports popularity.

Rugby League in New Zealand

Rugby league was introduced to New Zealand in 1907. A New Zealand team toured Great Britain that same year, helping to promote the sport back home.

Over time, New Zealand became highly competitive on the international stage and the sport continues to inspire young players across the country.

Rules of Rugby League

Rugby league is played between two teams of thirteen players, with the main objective being to score more points than the opposition within eighty minutes of play.

A try is worth four points and is scored by grounding the ball over the opponents try line.
