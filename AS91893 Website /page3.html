Maori Cuisine Recipes

Hangi

Ingredients (Serves 6)

  • 1 kg whole chichken (cut into pieces)
  • 500g lamb or pork
  • 4 large kumara(sweet potatoes), peeled and halved
  • 4 large potatoes, peeled and halved
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cabbage
  • Salt and pepper

Method:

  1. heat stones in a fire until very hot
  2. Prepare a pit in the ground
  3. Place meat and vegetables in basket
  4. Lower baskets onto hot stones
  5. Cover with cheese cloths and soil
  6. Cook for 2-3 hours

Rewana Bread

Ingredients:

  • 3 cups flour
  • 1 cup potato starter (rewana bug)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 cup warm water

Method:

  1. Mix all ingredients into dough
  2. Knead until smooth
  3. Leave and rise for several hours
  4. bake at 180°C for 30-40 minutes

Kai Moana

Ingredients:

  • 500g fresh fish or shellfish
  • Juice two lemons
  • 1 tsp salt

Method:

  1. clean seafood properly
  2. Lightly season with salt
  3. Add lemon juice
  4. Serve fresh or lightly cooked

Boil-up

Ingredients:

  • 1kg pork bones
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled, and cut into chunks
  • 1 bunch watercress
  • 2 cups flour (for dough-boys)
  • Water

Method:

  1. Boil pork bones in water for 1 hour
  2. Add potatoes and watercress into pot
  3. Make dough-boys and add to pot (just a mix of water and flour)
  4. Cook evrything until soft

Tongan Cuisine Recipes

Topai

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 can of coconut cream, and shredded coconut
  • 1 cup sugar

Method:

  1. Mix flour and water into dough
  2. Shape into small balls
  3. Boil in water until cooked
  4. Serve with coconut cream, sugar and shredded coconut

'Ota Ika

Ingredients:

  • 500g fresh fish
  • Juice of 3 limes
  • 1 cup of coconut cream
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 1 oninon (sliced)
  • 1/2 cucumber (chopped)

Method:

  1. Cut fish into cubes
  2. Soak in lime juice for 10-15 minutes
  3. Drain excess liquid
  4. Add vegetables
  5. Mix in with coconut cream
  6. Add salt and pepper

Lu Pulu

Ingredients:

  • 1 corned beef
  • 6 taro leaves
  • 1 cup of coconut cream

Method:

  1. Wrap corned beef into taro leaves
  2. Add coconut cream in with the corned beef
  3. Wrap the corn beef filled taro leaves in foil
  4. Cook in oven at 180°C for 1 hour until taro leaves are soft and dark green coloured

Feke

Ingredients:

  • 1kg octopus
  • 1 cup of coconut cream
  • 1 onion
  • Salt and pepper, and any spice

Method:

  1. Clean and cut octopus
  2. Boil until tender
  3. Add coconut cream and cooked onion
  4. Cmbine the two and simmer for 10 miuntes
