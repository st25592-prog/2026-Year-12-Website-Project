History & Traditional Foods

Explore how Maori and Tongan food traditions began and the dishes enjoyed today.

Cusine History

Maori History

Maori used earth ovens called hangi pits for centuries. Food like birds, fish, and kumara were cooked slowly underground.

Tongan History

Today, foods like corned beef (lu pulu) were introduced later but became part of everyday Tongan meals.

Popular Maori & Tongan Dishes

Traditional food made with culture, family, and heritage.

Maori Cuisine

Hangi

Food cooked underground using hot stones. Includes meat, kumara, and vegetables.

Rewana Bread

Traditional Maori sourdough bread made using natural fermentation.

Kai Moana

Fresh seafood such as fish, mussels, and shellfish gathered from the ocean.

Boil-up

A traditional soup with pork bones, sausages, dough-boys, watercress, and vegtables.

Tongan Cuisine

'Ota 'Ika

Raw fish marinated in coconut cream, lime juice, and mixed with fresh vegetables.

Lu Pulu

Corned beef wrapped in taro leaves and cooked in coconut cream.

Feke

Octopus cooked in coconut cream or grilled, with onions and spices. A popular seafood in Tonga.

Umu

Traditional earth oven cooking method using hot stones to cook food underground.

Topai

Soft dumplings made from flour and water, (similar to dough-boys) served with sweet coconut syrup.

Why Food Matters

In both Maori and Tongan cultures, food is more than just eating. It represents family, sharing, respect, and bringing people together during important gatherings and celebrations.
